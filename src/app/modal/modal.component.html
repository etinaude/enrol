 Syllabus Course Outline

Learning Outcomes

  1. describe the layers of a computer system, from hardware to the web (1, 4)
  2. convert standard data types into numeric formats and apply simple functions to them (1)
  3. identify basic gates and be able to relate them to truth tables and simple combinational circuits (1, 2)
  4. trace the execution of simple programs at the assembly language level (1, 2)
  5. produce pseudocode to specify solutions to simple programming tasks (1, 2, 3)
  6. describe the fundamental parts of an operating system and how they relate to applications (1, 4)
  7. demonstrate an understanding of network communications and computer security (1, 4)
  8. explain the central ideas in computer science research areas (such as artificial intelligence, computer graphics and theory) (1, 4)
  9. discuss ethical issues arising from the use of computers in society (4, 5, 6)
